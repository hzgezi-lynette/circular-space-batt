Circular Batteries
in Space

Enabling a Sustainable Space Economy | Oxford 2026

Context & Challenges

"Realising a circular space economy is a mission that transcends any single discipline. It requires a collective feedback loop between science, policy, and industry."

Building on the Oxford Expert Comment, we recognize that the path forward demands radical interdisciplinary and cross-sectoral collaboration. This workshop facilitates the dialogue needed to turn conceptual frameworks into actionable orbital solutions.

Space sustainability is a critical global concern as orbital occupancy grows exponentially. As of early 2024, there are over 9,000 active satellites; this is projected to reach 60,000 to 100,000 by 2030, creating unprecedented challenges for debris mitigation.

Battery components account for ~8% of the 1 million pieces of hazardous debris larger than 1cm. Abandoned batteries are active threats: they can explode due to degradation or thermal runaway. Historically, 30% to 40% of accidental fragmentation events are linked to energy system failures.

The Battery Lifecycle Gap

Battery systems account for 10% to 20% of a spacecraft’s dry mass. With launch costs to LEO at $2,000 - $5,000/kg, abandoning these components represents a massive loss of embedded energy and material investment.

Advancing Circularity & ISAM

This workshop explores systems designed for reuse and repair, aligning with the emerging ISAM market, projected to reach $14.3 billion by 2030. In-orbit replacement could extend asset life by 3 to 5 years.

Workshop Outputs

📄 Concept Paper

Defining strategic pathways for battery systems and design-for-serviceability protocols for orbital infrastructure.

⚖️ Policy Briefing

Recommendations on funding models and regulatory frameworks to catalyze the $14.3B ISAM economy.
